Tech Industry Funding Tips:

  • Most programs prefer you're not 100% industry-funded - diversify sources
  • Build relationships through internships, visiting researcher programs first
  • Understand each company's publication policies and IP stance
  • Leverage equipment/credits even if cash funding is limited
  • Consider their "AI for Good" or social impact initiatives for sociotechnical work
  • Many have specific diversity & inclusion funding tracks

State & Local Government Funding

Common State-Level Sources

  • State Science & Technology Initiatives: Many states have innovation funds
    • Examples: NYSTAR (New York), Mass Tech Collaborative, California Institute for Regenerative Medicine
    • Typically $25K-$500K for research projects
    • Often require in-state economic impact
  • Departments of Education:
    • K-12 technology integration research
    • Teacher professional development tech
    • Digital equity initiatives
    • Usually $50K-$250K
  • Departments of Health/Human Services:
    • Medicaid innovation (state-administered)
    • Public health technology pilots
    • Behavioral health tech initiatives
    • Range: $100K-$1M
  • Economic Development Agencies:
    • Innovation vouchers for SME partnerships
    • Cluster development grants
    • Workforce development tech
  • Transportation Departments:
    • Smart city pilots
    • Connected vehicle research
    • Equity in mobility studies

Finding State Opportunities: Check state government websites, subscribe to economic development newsletters, connect with state university system offices

Strategic Search Approaches

1. Comprehensive Search Strategy

  1. Start Broad:
    • Grants.gov for all federal opportunities
    • Foundation Directory Online for private foundations
    • Pivot or GrantForward for aggregated searches
  2. Set Up Alerts:
    • Keyword alerts: "sociotechnical," "human-computer," "digital society," etc.
    • Agency-specific RSS feeds
    • Foundation newsletter subscriptions
  3. Network Intelligence:
    • Join professional associations (ACM, IEEE, AAAI)
    • Attend program officer webinars
    • Follow funded PIs on social media
  4. Reverse Engineering:
    • Look at CVs of successful researchers in your area
    • Use NIH Reporter, NSF Award Search to find similar projects
    • Read acknowledgments in papers you admire

3. Creating Your Funding Map

  1. Identify Your Themes: What 3-5 keywords best describe your work?
  2. Map to Funders: Which agencies/foundations care about these themes?
  3. Understand Timelines: When are their deadlines? How long are review cycles?
  4. Build Relationships: Who are the program officers? Who has been funded?
  5. Plan Your Pipeline: Stagger submissions across multiple funders

Essential Resources & Tools

Primary Databases

  • Grants.gov: Comprehensive federal grant portal - set up saved searches and email alerts
  • Pivot: Global funding database (subscription) - includes federal, foundation, and international
  • GrantForward: Alternative to Pivot with good search capabilities
  • Foundation Directory Online: Best for private foundation research (subscription)
  • Instrumentl: AI-powered grant matching (subscription)

Federal Agency Tools

  • NIH RePORTER: Search all NIH-funded projects, see funding trends
  • NSF Award Search: All NSF awards with abstracts and outcomes
  • SBIR.gov: Central site for all SBIR/STTR opportunities
  • SAM.gov: System for Award Management - required for federal grants
